Welcome to Roteam.io — the all-in-one community management platform for Roblox. This guide walks you through creating your workspace, connecting your Roblox group, and configuring essential features.

## Create Your Account

To get started, you need a Roteam.io account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up">
    Head to [auth.roteam.io](https://auth.roteam.io) and click **Sign Up**. You can register with your email or sign in with Discord.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify your email">
    Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log in">
    Once verified, log in at [auth.roteam.io](https://auth.roteam.io) to access your dashboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Frame>

## Create a Workspace

A workspace is your team's home on Roteam.io. Each workspace connects to one Roblox group.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start workspace creation">
    From your dashboard, click **Create Workspace**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your workspace">
    Enter a name for your workspace. This is how your team will identify it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your Roblox group">
    Click **Connect Roblox Group** and authenticate with your Roblox account. Select the group you want to manage. You must be the owner or have admin permissions in the group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sync members">
    Once connected, Roteam.io will automatically sync your group members. This may take a moment depending on the size of your group.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/quickstart-create-workspace.png" alt="Create workspace dialog" />
</Frame>

<Tip>
  Member data is synced periodically from your Roblox group. You can trigger a manual sync from **Settings > Workspace Info** at any time.
</Tip>

## Set Up Permissions

Before configuring features, set up permission groups so the right people have access to the right tools.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings** from the sidebar, then select the **Members & Roles** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create permission groups">
    Click **Create Permission Group** and give it a name (e.g., "Moderators", "HR Team", "Managers").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign permissions">
    For each group, toggle the permissions you want to grant. Permissions are organized by feature — Announcements, Activity, Leaderboard, Courses, Knowledgebase, Applications, Discipline, and more.

    Common permission types include:
    - **View** — See the feature and its data
    - **Create** — Add new records (announcements, strikes, etc.)
    - **Edit** — Modify existing records
    - **Delete** — Remove records
    - **Manage** — Full control including settings
    - **Approve** — Approve requests (promotions, time off, etc.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add members to groups">
    Assign users to permission groups. A user can belong to multiple groups. The workspace owner (rank 255) automatically has all permissions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/quickstart-permissions.png" alt="Permission groups settings" />
</Frame>

<Note>
  Permission changes take effect immediately. Review your groups carefully before granting **Manage** or **Delete** permissions.
</Note>

## Configure Activity Tracking

Activity Tracking records time your members spend in-game, including AFK detection and chat message counts.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up tracked roles">
    Go to **Settings > Members & Roles** and mark which Roblox roles should be tracked. Only members in tracked roles will appear on leaderboards and have their activity recorded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the game tracking script">
    To collect in-game data, you need to add the Roteam tracking module to your Roblox experience. Go to **Settings > Game Tracking** and follow the installation instructions to add the script to your game.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify tracking">
    After installing the script, join your game and spend a few minutes in-game. Then check the **Activity** page in your dashboard — you should see your session appear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/quickstart-activity-tracking.png" alt="Activity tracking setup" />
</Frame>

<Tip>
  Activity data includes total minutes, AFK minutes, and chat messages. The heatmap on each member's activity page shows the last 365 days of activity at a glance.
</Tip>

## Set Up Auto Ranking

Auto Ranking automatically promotes or demotes members in your Roblox group based on their activity and performance.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Advancement settings">
    Go to **Settings > Advancement** or find the Auto Ranking configuration in the **Advancement** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Auto Ranking">
    Toggle **Auto Ranking** on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your Roblox API key">
    Enter your Roblox Open Cloud API key. This allows Roteam.io to update ranks in your Roblox group automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define rank conditions">
    For each rank, set the criteria for promotion. For example:
    - **Trainee → Junior Staff**: 500+ activity minutes and 10+ sessions attended
    - **Junior Staff → Staff**: 2,000+ minutes and 30+ sessions

    You can use activity minutes, session counts, and other metrics as conditions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set cooldowns">
    Optionally configure a minimum number of days between promotions to prevent rapid rank changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/quickstart-auto-ranking.png" alt="Auto ranking configuration" />
</Frame>

<Warning>
  Make sure your Roblox API key has the correct permissions to manage group members. Without proper permissions, rank changes will fail silently.
</Warning>

## Create Sessions & Events

Sessions let you schedule training events, meetings, and activities for your team.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Sessions">
    Navigate to **Sessions & Events** from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create an event">
    Click **Create Event** and fill in:
    - **Title** — Name of the session (e.g., "Weekly Training")
    - **Date & Time** — When the event takes place
    - **Type** — Training, meeting, interview, etc.
    - **Roblox Game** — Link to the game where the event will happen
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign roles">
    Assign a **Host** and optionally a **Co-host**. You can also create custom session roles for participants (e.g., "Trainer", "Supervisor").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up recurring events">
    For regular sessions, enable **Recurring** and choose the frequency — daily, weekly, biweekly, monthly, or weekdays. Set an optional end date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure server settings">
    Set server lock and unlock windows to control when members can join the game server for the event.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configure Time Off

Set up leave management so members can request time off and managers can approve or deny requests.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define leave types">
    Go to **Settings** and configure your leave types (e.g., Vacation, Sick Leave, Personal Leave, LOA).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up approvers">
    Configure which permission groups or users can approve time-off requests.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the workflow">
    Submit a test time-off request to verify the approval workflow is working correctly. Navigate to **Time Off**, click **Request Time Off**, select a leave type, date range, and reason, then submit.
  </Step>
</Steps>
