The Knowledgebase is your workspace's central library for documentation. Create articles, organize them into collections, and give your team a searchable repository of policies, guides, and training materials.

## Browsing the Knowledgebase

Navigate to **Knowledgebase** from the sidebar. The main page features:
- A **search bar** for finding articles by title, content, tags, or author
- **Pinned articles** displayed prominently at the top
- **Collections** shown as cards with article counts

</Frame>

Click on a collection to browse all articles within it, or use the search bar to find specific content.

## Searching Articles

The search bar provides instant results as you type, searching across:
- Article titles
- Article excerpts and content
- Tags
- Author names

## Reading an Article

Click on any article card to read the full content. Articles display:
- Title and excerpt
- Author(s)
- Collection and tags
- Full article content with rich text formatting
- Last updated date

## Creating an Article

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Create Article">
    From the Knowledgebase page, click **Create Article**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write the article">
    - **Title** — A clear, descriptive headline
    - **Excerpt** — A short summary shown on the article card
    - **Content** — The full article body. The rich text editor supports:
      - Headings, bold, italic, and lists
      - Code blocks
      - Links and embeds
      - Images
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set metadata">
    - **Collection** — Choose which collection the article belongs to
    - **Tags** — Add tags for filtering and organization
    - **Status** — Set to **Draft** (only visible to admins) or **Published** (visible to all)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to create the article. If published, it's immediately available to your team.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pinning Articles

Pin important articles so they appear at the top of the Knowledgebase:

1. Open the article
2. Click the **Pin** button (or use the three-dot menu)
3. Pinned articles are marked with a pin icon and displayed in the pinned section

<Tip>
  Pin your most frequently referenced articles like onboarding guides, code of conduct, or current policies.
</Tip>

## Managing Collections

Collections are categories that organize your articles. Each collection has a name, description, and icon.

### Creating a Collection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Knowledgebase settings">
    Click the **Settings** button on the Knowledgebase page, or go to **Settings > Knowledgebase**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a collection">
    Click **Add Collection** and provide:
    - **Name** — Collection title (e.g., "Policies", "Training Guides", "SOPs")
    - **Description** — What kind of articles this collection contains
    - **Icon** — Choose an icon for the collection card
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Save the collection. It will appear on the Knowledgebase main page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Tags

Tags add another layer of organization beyond collections.

1. Go to **Settings > Knowledgebase**
2. Under **Tags**, click **Add Tag**
3. Enter a tag name and select a color
4. Tags are available when creating or editing articles

## Article Status

- **Draft** — The article is work-in-progress and only visible to users with management permissions
- **Published** — The article is live and visible to all workspace members with Knowledgebase view permissions

To change an article's status, edit the article and toggle the status field.

<Note>
  Deleting a collection does not delete its articles. Articles in a deleted collection will become uncategorized.
</Note>
